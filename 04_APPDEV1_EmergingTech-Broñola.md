## Purpose
At its core, AR and VR bridges the gap between the physcial world and the virtual world. Unlike traditional computers which only sees the technology on screens, this emerging technology creates an environment that blends technology with the real world.
<details>
  <summary><b>Augmented Reality</b></summary>
  
  * This technology overlays digital elements onto the real world such as, 3D models, animations, text, images, etc. Its purpose is to enhance real-world conditions using technology without isolating the user from interacting with the real-world environment.

</details>
<details>
  <summary><b>Virtual Reality</b></summary>
  
  * This technology creates a fully digital environment wherein the user can interact with. Its purpose is to replicate experiences that are too dangerus to do in real life, too expensive, or physicaly impossible to replicate in real life.

</details>

## Usage
<details>
  <summary><b>In Education</b></summary>
  
  * AR/VR creates virtual classroms that let students immerse themselves in a new world by making lessons more interactive and engaging. VR let students explore hirtorical sites, and experiment on virtual labs. Moreover, AR brings complex 3D models to life, especially those that are physically impossible to see in full such as planetary systems and overall architectural designs.

</details>
<details>
  <summary><b>In the IT Industry</b></summary>
  
  * AR is actively utilized by enterprise IT and manufacturing to manage complex systems and wiring. Additionally, platforms like Vuforia provide AR solutions that overlay 3D work instructions directly onto server racks and mechanical equipment, enhancing IT technicians capabiliies to perform complex repairs with real-time guidance through technology.
  *  Spatial computing (AR/VR Technology) removes the limitations of remote IT work by creating virtual offices. Platforms developed by companies like Meta helps in creating a digital workspace that mimic real-world environments even in remote areas. Colleagues can brainstorm, share ideas, and collaborate in real-time remotely.

</details>

<h2>Real-World Examples</h2>
<details>
  <summary><b>In Education</b></summary>
  
  * The University of Waterloo used VR to help students that cannot attend physical fieldtrips due to physical limitations experience reality field trips using Spongy Bog 360 VR Field Trip.
  * At Boston College, students coded an interactive VR game based on James Joyce’s novel Ulysses (titled "Joycestick"). This allowed literature students to directly experience scenes from the book, interact with historically accurate objects, and experience the novel in real-time, which transforms them from passive readers into active participants.

</details>
<details>
  <summary><b>In the IT Industry</b></summary>
  
  * Thyssenkrupp used Microsoft’s Dynamics 365 Remote Assist on HoloLens headsets to support field service engineers. A remote IT or engineering expert can mark in a live video feed with a digital pen, if the expert pointed to the exact component or wire that requires maintenance, it will then appear as a hologram in the field technician's view. 
  * Enterprise IT departments deploy AR headsets (such as those provided by ThirdEye) to address training defficiencies. Instead of forcing junior IT staff to read through long paper manuals while fixing hardware errors inside server rooms, a remote expert uses AR to stream in to give real-time instructions and visual indications hands-free.

</details>
